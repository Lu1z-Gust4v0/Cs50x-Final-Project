Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies or "digital cash" are a new mean of payment that are not controlled by any central entity. Their transactions are made through peer-to-peer networks that do not rely on third parties.

Block Chain

Block Chain is a digital "book" that stores the data of all transactions ever made in the past. Any person can participate and verify its data at any time.

Smart Contracts

Smart Contract is an algorithm stored in a block chain that executes itself when a certain event happens. Its major advantage is to provide security for transactions, since its functionality cannot be alterated.

Wallets

Wallets are computer or mobile apps that allow the people to store their cryptocurrencies. The wallets are protected by encryption that prevents hackers from stealing your coins.

Web 3.0

Web 3.0 is a new web paradigm that enables users and machines to interact with data, value and other counterparties via peer-to-peer networks without the need for third parties.
